ChatGPT Follows AI Companion Platforms and Launches Group Chat Feature

Collaborative AI Conversations Now Available in Select Regions

OpenAI officially launches Group Chats in ChatGPT, allowing users in select regions to engage in multi-user conversations with the AI-powered language model, following the successful trend set by AI companion platforms.

San Francisco, November 16, 2025 - In an exciting development, OpenAI has officially launched Group Chats in ChatGPT, allowing users in select regions to engage in multi-user conversations with the AI-powered language model. The feature, initially hinted at by AI influencers on social media, is now available as a limited pilot program in Japan, New Zealand, South Korea, and Taiwan.

Collaborative Conversations with ChatGPT

The new Group Chat feature empowers users to incorporate ChatGPT into their existing group chats, enabling dynamic interactions between users and the AI. This feature allows participants to send messages to each other and ChatGPT simultaneously, either online or through the mobile app, providing a unique collaborative experience. OpenAI describes this as a step towards transforming ChatGPT into a shared space for collaboration and interaction.

Participants can engage in real-time discussions, brainstorming sessions, or collaborative problem-solving with ChatGPT as an active participant. The AI responds contextually to group conversations, understanding when to contribute and when to remain silent, creating a more natural flow of discussion.

AI Companions Already Leading the Way

While ChatGPT's group chat feature is new to mainstream AI assistants, AI companion platforms have been pioneering multi-user and group chat features for some time. Several leading AI companion platforms already offer robust group chat capabilities:

Dream Companion (9.5/10)

Dream Companion leads the way with sophisticated group interaction features, enabling users to create shared experiences with multiple AI personalities. The platform's exceptional rating reflects its outstanding approach to collaborative AI conversations and community building.

Secret Share AI (9.2/10)

Secret Share AI offers advanced group chat capabilities with multilingual support, allowing users to engage in dynamic multi-user conversations across different languages. The platform's high rating demonstrates its commitment to creating inclusive and feature-rich collaborative experiences.

Aviosa AI (8.0/10)

Aviosa AI provides dedicated group chat functionality with multi-character scenes, enabling users to create complex interactions between multiple AI personalities. The platform supports diverse group dynamics, making it ideal for collaborative storytelling and shared AI experiences.

"Our models have a lot more room to shine than today's experiences show." - Keyan Zhang, Technical Staffer at OpenAI

Pilot Program and Technical Advances

While this innovative feature is currently limited to a few regions, it is built on OpenAI's internal experiments, which showed promising potential for real-time, collaborative AI interactions. Keyan Zhang, a technical staffer at OpenAI, noted that the model's performance during initial tests exceeded expectations, highlighting the transformative potential of this technology.

The pilot program focuses on gathering user feedback and refining the group chat experience before a broader rollout. OpenAI is monitoring usage patterns, technical performance, and user satisfaction to inform future development decisions.

Privacy and Security Features

OpenAI has emphasized privacy in the design of the Group Chat feature. Conversations held in these group settings are separate from ChatGPT's memory system, ensuring that no data from these discussions is used for training or personalization. This approach addresses growing concerns about AI data usage and user privacy.

Additionally, participants under 18 are protected from sensitive content, with parental controls allowing for further customization of access. These safety measures reflect lessons learned from the broader AI companion industry, where platforms have developed sophisticated content filtering and age verification systems.

Looking Ahead: Broader Applications and Developer Access

The rollout of Group Chats marks a significant step in OpenAI's journey to enhance AI interaction by creating a more collaborative environment. While the feature is currently limited to the ChatGPT product environment, it sparks curiosity about potential future applications and accessibility for developers.

As OpenAI continues to refine and expand this feature, it could pave the way for new multi-user AI applications across various sectors, from education to enterprise collaboration to creative projects.

Implications for Enterprise AI Deployment

For enterprise teams already leveraging AI, this feature introduces a new layer of collaboration. It could influence how generative models are integrated into workflows, particularly in areas like research, content creation, and ideation. With the pilot currently limited to specific regions, its design offers insights for AI engineers and data leaders worldwide about the future of AI-driven collaboration.

The group chat functionality opens possibilities for virtual brainstorming sessions, collaborative document editing with AI assistance, and team-based problem-solving enhanced by AI insights.
