Interactive Stories Replace Open-Ended Chat for Kids on Character AI

Platform Transforms Teen Experience from AI Conversations to Pre-Written Narrative Adventures

Effective November 25, 2025, Character AI has fundamentally changed its approach to users under 18, replacing open-ended conversations with interactive stories featuring content from Disney, PBS Kids, and Nickelodeon.

Effective November 25, 2025, Character AI has fundamentally changed its approach to users under 18, replacing open-ended conversations with interactive stories. While adult users retain full chatbot access, Character AI for younger users no longer functions as a chatbot. Instead, teen users now experience pre-written narrative adventures where their role is to make multiple-choice decisions that guide the story forward.

Character AI's Most Significant Product Pivot

This shift represents Character AI's most significant product pivot since founding, transforming the teen experience from free-flowing AI conversation into structured, curated content. The company states this better protects younger users while maintaining entertainment value.

Character AI's teen-focused product now resembles interactive fiction more than AI chat. Users select from pre-approved story scenarios and navigate through predetermined narrative branches. Each decision point offers limited choices that advance the plot in pre-planned directions.

"This move essentially creates two separate products under the Character AI brand. Adults get the original conversational AI experience. Teens get what amounts to a digital storybook with interactive elements." - Industry Analysts

Key Restrictions for Users Under 18

The new restrictions fundamentally change how younger users can interact with the platform:

  • No custom messages: Users cannot type custom messages or prompts
  • Pre-written dialogue: All dialogue comes from pre-written story content
  • Scripted responses: Character responses follow scripted paths rather than AI generation
  • Pre-moderated content: Content is pre-moderated and approved before publication
  • No unexpected conversations: No way to steer conversations into unexpected territory

Brand Partnerships and Story Content

Character AI has partnered with children's media brands to provide story content, including Disney, PBS Kids, and Nickelodeon properties. The first wave includes stories featuring popular characters like:

  • Bluey - The popular Australian animated series
  • SpongeBob SquarePants - The iconic Nickelodeon character
  • Dora the Explorer - The beloved educational adventure series

These partnerships ensure that younger users have access to familiar, age-appropriate content while the platform maintains strict safety standards.

Dramatically Different Teen Interface

The teen interface looks dramatically different from the adult product. Gone is the text input field where users compose messages. Instead, users see story text appear on screen followed by branching choice buttons. The aesthetic resembles choose-your-own-adventure books more than chat applications.

This design choice ensures that younger users cannot accidentally or intentionally engage with AI-generated content that hasn't been pre-approved by Character AI's moderation team.

Industry Impact and User Base

For context, Character AI reached 1 million downloads in its first week of launch and currently counts 4.2 million monthly mobile active users in the United States alone. The platform previously allowed users of all ages to engage in unrestricted roleplay with AI characters.

Industry analysts have noted the move essentially creates two separate products under the Character AI brand. Adults get the original conversational AI experience. Teens get what amounts to a digital storybook with interactive elements.

This follows Character AI's earlier announcement to ban minors from open-ended conversations following multiple lawsuits linking the platform to teenage suicides.
