Understanding Emotional AI

Emotional AI, also known as affective computing, is designed to detect, interpret, and respond to human emotions. This technology uses data from facial expressions, voice intonations, and physiological signals to gauge emotional states. As companies like Affectiva advance this technology, we see a push towards integrating these capabilities in various sectors, from customer service to mental health support. Many modern AI romantic companions now incorporate emotional recognition features.

Affectiva, now part of the iMotions group, maintains what they describe as the "world's largest emotion database," built from 18 years of data spanning 90 countries with 17.4 million face videos and 8 billion facial frames analyzed.

The IRL Pushback

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. We're now starting to see Gen-Z push back against algorithms in favor of the weird and wonderful world of in real-life (IRL) connections.

  • Gen-Z is actively seeking more community events, spontaneous meetups and pop-up activations
  • More young people are rejecting dating apps in favor of running clubs, book clubs and board games
  • Gen-Z is embracing digital detox and spending more time outdoors "touching grass"
  • In China, young people are escaping cities to youth retirement villages seeking simpler life and better work-life balance

In 2026, more brands will need to adapt to Gen-Z and Gen Alpha's desire to unplug from social media and AI platforms. Not being chronically online will become the new status signal. We might be witnessing the beginnings of a new social divide between those who outsource emotional intimacy to machines and those who reject algorithms in search of human connections again.
